Understanding SQLite & ACID Properties

1. What are transactions?
A transaction is a group of database operations executed as a single unit. Either all changes within a transaction happen, or none do, ensuring data integrity.
2. What are ACID properties?
  • Atomicity: Ensures that a transaction is fully completed or not at all.
  • Consistency: Guarantees that the database remains valid before and after a transaction.
  • Isolation: Prevents transactions from affecting each other.
  • Durability: Ensures that committed data remains even after system failures.
3. Suppose you do not have transactions. Is that system useful? Why?
Without transactions, data integrity is at risk. If a failure occurs mid-operation, only partial changes might be saved, leading to corruption.
4. What properties does your file system have?
Most file systems provide some level of atomicity, but they lack built-in ACID guarantees. File systems prioritize performance over transactional consistency.
5. Suppose you do not have "A" in ACID. What happens? When is it ok?
Without Atomicity, partial transactions may be saved, causing inconsistent data. It might be acceptable in logging systems where partial data is better than none.
6. Suppose you do not have "C" in ACID. What happens? When is it ok?
Without Consistency, data may violate rules, leading to corruption. Some NoSQL databases allow temporary inconsistency for better performance.
7. Suppose you do not have "I" in ACID. What happens? When is it ok?
Without Isolation, multiple transactions can interfere, causing unexpected results. It’s fine in scenarios where real-time speed is prioritized over accuracy.
8. Suppose you do not have "D" in ACID. What happens? When is it ok?
Without Durability, committed data might be lost after system crashes. It’s acceptable for temporary data like caches.

Conclusion

Understanding transactions and ACID properties is essential when working with databases. Choosing the right system depends on the use case, whether it's performance, reliability, or scalability.
